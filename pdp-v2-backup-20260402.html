A note from the team
Why we don't use stainless steel.

We get asked this a lot. Some customers see a stainless steel filter on another site and wonder if ours is lesser. We understand the instinct — steel feels premium. So we want to be straight with you about why we chose a different path.

The housing doesn't filter your water. The cartridge does. Every dollar we spend on the housing is a dollar not spent on the 5-stage nano filtration media inside. We chose to engineer the part that touches your water — and keep the shell simple, light, and replaceable.

Our housing is high-grade ABS — the same food-grade material used in Breville coffee machines, dental equipment, and medical devices. It's BPA-free, doesn't corrode in constant contact with water, and weighs 180g. A stainless steel housing weighs 400g+ and puts real stress on your tap aerator thread over time.

ABS is designed to be opened. You'll swap your cartridge every 2–3 months. Stainless steel housings over-tighten, cross-thread, and seize up. Some need tools. Ours twists open by hand, every time, even after a year.

And if anything ever breaks — valve, lever, adapter, housing — we replace it. Free. For life. That's the Forever Fresh Guarantee. No receipt. No time limit. Just email us.

Stainless steel gives you the feeling of durability. We'd rather give you the reality of it.

How it works

Six stages. Pure water, then better water.

Stages 1–5 in every EcoPro. Stage 6 is exclusive to the SMR™.

Polypropylene Mesh
Captures sediment and microorganisms before they reach the carbon block.
Stage 2
Carbon Block

Questions

Common questions.

Will it fit my tap?
+
Adapters are included free and cover 95% of Australian taps — kitchen mixers, gooseneck taps, basin mixers, pillar taps, laundry taps and more. Doesn't fit pull-out spray taps or square/angular taps. Not sure? Send us a photo to hello@tappwater.com.au — we'll confirm before you order and send the right adapter free.
Can I switch between filtered and unfiltered water?
+
Yes — there's a built-in diverter lever. Flick up for filtered drinking water, down for full-pressure unfiltered. No unscrewing required. This means you get full flow for washing dishes without filtering water you don't need to drink, which extends cartridge life.
⚠  Can I use it with hot or boiling water?
+
No — the EcoPro is cold water only. Hot or boiling water damages the filter media and can cause bacterial growth. Always switch to lever-down (bypass) mode before running warm water. This is standard for all activated carbon tap filters, not just ours.
What's the difference between the Compact and the SMR?
+
Same 5-stage filtration core. Same lab results. Same contaminant removal. The SMR adds a 6th stage — Swedish Mineral Rock™ — that puts calcium, magnesium, and potassium back into your filtered water. The SMR cartridge also lasts 3 months vs 2 for the Compact. Clean water: get the Compact. Filtered and remineralised: get the SMR.
How often do I replace the cartridge?
+
Compact: every 2 months or 220L. SMR: every 3 months or 1,200L. Replace on schedule — don't wait for a taste change. An exhausted cartridge still flows but stops filtering, and there's a risk of bacterial growth after the rated lifespan. Annual Pack gives you all refills upfront so you never have to think about it.
Does it reduce fluoride?
+
70% fluoride reduction — most carbon filters remove 0%. We publish the honest number alongside the 98% chlorine result. We won't overclaim. For >99% fluoride removal, look at our RO Countertop.
Does it remove chlorine taste and smell?
+
Yes. 98% chlorine removal — the chlorine taste and smell go with it. This is often the first thing people notice, usually from the first glass.
Will it slow my tap pressure?
+
In filtered mode there's a slight flow reduction — normal for any filter (water takes longer to pass through media). In unfiltered mode (lever down) your tap runs at full pressure. Works on most Australian mains water. Minimum pressure required: 5 psi (0.35 bar).
What do I do with old cartridges?
+
Separate the carbon block from the plastic housing. The carbon block goes to organic/green waste (or crush it into soil — it aerates and retains water). The plastic housing goes to recycling. For a second life: dry the carbon block, put it in a sock, and use it as a natural odour absorber for a fridge, drawer, or car.
I'm a renter. Can I use this?
+
Designed for it. Screws onto your existing tap aerator in 60 seconds. No drilling, no plumber, no damage to the property. Take it with you when you move — and let us know if the new tap needs a different adapter. We'll send it free.
What if it doesn't fit or I'm not happy?

30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked. If it doesn't fit your tap and we can't supply an adapter, we'll refund you in full. If you're unsure before buying, send us a photo — we'd rather tell you it won't work than take your money.
