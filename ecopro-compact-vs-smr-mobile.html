At a glance

The key differences

Same filtration core, same chrome finish, same 60-second install. Here's where they split.

SMR adds minerals back

The SMR's 6th stage — Swedish Mineral Rock™ — adds calcium, magnesium, and potassium after filtration. The Compact delivers pure, clean water without minerals.

6-stage vs 5-stage filtration

Both share the same 5-stage core (93% PFAS, 98% chlorine, 70% fluoride). The SMR adds a 6th mineral enrichment stage for superior overall benefits.

3-month vs 2-month cartridge

SMR cartridges last 3 months / 1,200L — only 4 changes per year. Compact cartridges last 2 months / 220L — 6 changes per year.

Compact is smaller

The Compact (122 × 68 × 58mm) tucks away behind the tap — great for bathroom sinks and tight spaces. The SMR (90 × 100mm) is a larger chrome cylinder.

Water pressure

The Compact handles all pressure levels. The SMR performs best with higher water pressure — ideal for most kitchen taps.

$40 price difference

Compact starts at $109.99, SMR at $149.99. Annual refill cost is $159.99 vs $179.99 — about 5¢/day difference.

Both filters are chrome, both look great on any tap, and both install in 60 seconds with no tools.

What sets the SMR apart

Swedish Mineral Rock™ — the 6th stage

Both filters remove 100+ contaminants. The SMR then passes water through Swedish Mineral Rock™, adding essential minerals back for balanced, better-tasting hydration.
